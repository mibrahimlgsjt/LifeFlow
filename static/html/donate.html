The Donation Process

Simple steps to follow when donating blood

1

Registration

Complete a donor registration form with your personal information and medical history.

2

Health Check

Our medical staff will check your temperature, blood pressure, pulse and hemoglobin levels.

3

Donation

The actual blood donation typically takes about 8-10 minutes. You'll be seated comfortably.

4

Recovery

After donating, you'll be provided refreshments to help replenish fluids and nutrients.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about blood donation

Who can donate blood?
Most people who are in good health, at least 17 years old (16 with parental consent in some states), and weigh at least 110 pounds can donate blood. However, there are some eligibility criteria based on your medical history, travel history, and current health status.
How often can I donate blood?
You can donate whole blood every 56 days (about 8 weeks). If you donate red blood cells using an automated process (apheresis), you can donate every 112 days (about 16 weeks). For platelets, you can donate every 7 days, up to 24 times per year.
Is blood donation safe?
Yes, blood donation is very safe. All equipment used for blood donation is sterile and used only once. There is no risk of contracting any disease by donating blood. Some donors may experience slight dizziness or bruising at the needle site, but serious complications are extremely rare.
How long does the donation process take?
The entire blood donation process from registration to recovery takes about an hour. The actual blood donation typically takes about 8-10 minutes. For platelet donation, the process can take up to 2 hours.
What should I do before donating blood?
Before donating blood, it's recommended to eat a healthy meal, be well hydrated, get a good night's sleep, and avoid fatty foods. Wear comfortable clothing with sleeves that can be rolled up above the elbow. Bring a valid ID and list of medications you're taking.
